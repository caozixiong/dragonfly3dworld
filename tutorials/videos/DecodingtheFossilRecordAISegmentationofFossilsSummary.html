Decoding the Fossil Record: The Benefits of AI in the Segmentation of Fossils

Summary

Franklin from the Denver Museum of Nature and Science, discusses the application of AI, specifically within Dragonfly, for the segmentation of fossils. The presentation addresses the unique challenges posed by fossil data, which often includes inconsistent specimens, low contrast, and varied matrices [00:00:15]. The research aims to leverage AI to expedite the segmentation process, facilitating the analysis of a larger volume of scan data [00:00:48].

Key Points:

  • Digital Preparation: The process involves scanning fossils, extracting bone structures, and reconstructing them, sometimes including endocasts for analyzing brain cavities [00:01:56].
  • Challenges in Fossil Segmentation: Fossils exhibit inconsistency, low contrast due to surrounding rock, compression, and disarticulation [00:02:41].
  • Fossil Types: The presentation covers various fossil types, including compression fossils, coprolites (fossilized feces), and concretions, each presenting unique segmentation challenges [00:04:13].
  • Classical vs. Deep Learning: Classical machine learning is preferred for specimens with clear material separation, while deep learning is applied to more complex structures [00:05:20].
  • Hardware Specifications: The research was conducted using a machine with specific specifications, emphasizing the importance of considering hardware capabilities [00:05:59].
  • Model Architectures: Classical models used are Random Forests, and deep learning models are based on the U-Net architecture with five to seven layers [00:06:15].
  • Best Practices: The presenter recommends using the segmentation wizard in Dragonfly, prioritizing quality in training datasets, and utilizing 2D models for time efficiency [00:06:31].
  • Specific Applications:
    • Zoological Specimens: Classical machine learning is effective due to clear separation between bone, scales, and soft tissue [00:08:05].
    • Prepared Fossils: Both classical and deep learning are used to map sutures for bone-by-bone segmentation [00:08:48].
    • Compression Fossils: Deep learning captures subtle details, though challenges remain with artifacts [00:10:16].
    • Coprolites: Deep learning aids in extracting bone material and separating concreted masses [00:12:32].
    • Concretions: Deep learning is used to tag items within concretions, even in very low-quality data [00:15:12].
  • Plaster Jackets: Classical machine learning is used to separate plaster jackets from the matrix, improving efficiency [00:16:41].

The presentation concludes by emphasizing the importance of creative and efficient use of AI tools in Dragonfly for fossil segmentation, regardless of data quality [00:17:41].
