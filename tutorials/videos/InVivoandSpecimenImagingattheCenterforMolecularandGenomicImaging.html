In Vivo and Specimen Imaging at the Center for Molecular and Genomic Imaging

This presentation discusses in vivo and specimen imaging techniques at the UC Davis Center for Molecular and Genomic Imaging. The presenter, Doug, a principal research scientist, details the center's work with micro-CT imaging, focusing on two primary applications:

I. In Vivo Imaging of COVID-19 in Mice

The research aims to establish an in vivo model for Post-Acute Sequelae of SARS-CoV-2 infection (PASC), also known as long COVID, using genetically humanized mice. Key aspects include:

  • Animal Model: Novel genetically modified mice with humanized ACE2, TMPRSS2, and furin genes [00:08:45].
  • Imaging Technique: Respiratory-gated micro-CT lung imaging [00:11:19].
  • Image Analysis: Deep learning model using a 3D Unet architecture for lung segmentation [00:13:46].
  • Deep Learning Parameters:
    • Model: 3D Unet [00:13:46]
    • Data Augmentation: Utilized [00:13:46]
    • Image Mask Size: Specified [00:13:53]
    • Training Data: Six training sets at both end-expiration and inspiration [00:14:11]
  • Observed Results: Initial findings indicate decreased lung volume in infected animals at day five post-infection [00:16:28].

II. Specimen Imaging of Orchid Bee Brains

This section focuses on the use of micro-CT for studying sexual dimorphism in the brains of neotropical orchid bees. Key details:

  • Research Focus: Investigating anatomical differences in bee brains to understand behavioral diversity between male and female bees [00:19:13].
  • Imaging Technique: Micro-CT imaging with iodine and ethanol staining for contrast enhancement [00:20:41].
  • Image Analysis: Manual segmentation of nine brain regions [00:20:57].
  • Findings:
    • Male bees show enlargement in brain regions related to visualization [00:23:21].
    • Female bees exhibit enlargement in brain regions associated with learning and memory [00:23:37].
