Three-Dimensional Visualization of Bone Tissue Vascular and Cellular Spaces

Presentation by Mary from The Ohio State University, discussing the use of Dragonfly for visualizing bone tissue.

Key Topics:

  • Introduction to Bone Quality: Bone quality is affected by mechanical adaptation and physiological modification [00:03:40].
  • Bone Remodeling: Bone adapts to mechanical demand via osteocytes, triggering bone removal (osteoclasts) or formation (osteoblasts) [00:03:49].
  • Modeling vs. Remodeling:
    • Modeling: Separate removal or formation of bone [00:04:15].
    • Remodeling: Activity within cortical bone, replacing old or damaged bone [00:05:25].
  • Age-Associated Bone Loss: Bone resorption outpaces formation, starting as early as the 20s (trabecular) and 30s (cortical) [00:06:42].
  • Clinical Evaluation: DEXA scans are used, but under-predict fracture risk [00:07:54].
  • Bone Microstructure: Hierarchical system supporting bone strength, including cross-sectional geometry, trabecular bone, porosity, osteons, and more [00:08:42].
  • 3D Imaging Methods:
    • Micro-CT: High-resolution imaging [00:11:01].
    • Synchrotron Radiation Micro-CT: Sub-micron resolution, performed at specialized facilities [00:11:37].
    • Confocal Laser Scanning Microscopy: Precise resolution in X, Y, and Z planes, but limited depth [00:12:18].
  • Project 1: Mechanical Loading History:
    • Goal: To determine if pores produced by high strain targeted remodeling are morphologically different from pores produced by lower strain disuse mode remodeling [00:15:54].
    • Methodology:
      • Compared femoral neck (weight-bearing) to rib (unloaded) in human subjects [00:16:09].
      • Micro-CT scans at 6.4 .097 microns [00:18:27].
      • Used Dragonfly for reassembling cut femoral neck sections [00:19:24].
      • Adaptive thresholding to isolate pore structures [00:20:10].
      • 3D morphometry in CT analyzer [00:22:26].
    • Findings: Pore morphology varies with strain; dysregulated with age [00:22:56].
  • Project 2: Opioid-Driven Bone Loss: Used a rabbit model to visualize bone changes due to opioid use [00:24:38].

Technical Details:

  1. Image Acquisition: Micro-CT scans were performed at varying resolutions, including sub-micron resolution using synchrotron radiation [00:11:01], [00:11:37].
  2. Software Used:
    • Dragonfly: For 3D visualization and orientation of image stacks [00:19:24].
    • ImageJ/Fiji: For developing macros (e.g., Pore Extractor 3D) and image processing [00:18:50].
    • CT Analyzer: For morphometry analysis [00:19:03], [00:22:26].
  3. Segmentation Techniques: Morphological smoothing and binary operations were used [00:13:09]. Adaptive thresholding was employed to isolate pore structures [00:20:10].
  4. Data Analysis: 3D morphometry was conducted using CT Analyzer [00:22:26].
