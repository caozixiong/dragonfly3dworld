About Dragonfly Software [00:03:37]

Dragonfly is an image analysis software package designed for:

  • Visualization of images from microscopy and tomography tools
  • Measurement and inspection in 2D, 3D, and 4D (time series data)
  • Image processing and segmentation
  • Deep learning tools for segmentation with limited data
  • Quantitative analysis
  • Animations and reporting
  • Data analysis

The software is free for non-commercial use, making it particularly valuable for academics. The academic license is not a limited version but the full software, identical to the commercial license. This approach stems from the software's academic origins and our commitment to supporting research.

For commercial users, licenses can be either annual or perpetual (one-time payment). We also offer:

  • Maintenance and support plans ($2,500/year for priority academic support)
  • Training (online or on-site)
  • Image analysis services
  • Viz Server product for remote multi-user access

Terminology [00:10:12]

Dragonfly uses specific terminology that might be unfamiliar if you're coming from a different software background:

  • Images, ROIs (Region of Interest), and Multi-ROIs: These are structured grids represented by a grid in 3D space
  • Meshes: Triangular elements in 3D space representing surfaces, separate from structured grids
  • Windows Leveling: The term used for contrast adjustment (terminology from medical imaging origins)

It's important to understand that meshes and images are completely separate in Dragonfly. If you move or translate image data, you can instruct the associated mesh to follow the same transformation by right-clicking and selecting the appropriate option.

Basic Shortcuts [00:12:02]

Essential mouse shortcuts to know:

  • Pan/Move: Press both left and right mouse buttons simultaneously while dragging (works in both 2D and 3D windows)
  • Zoom: Press down the mouse wheel and drag forward/backward
  • Contrast adjustment: Hold the Control key and use the right mouse button while dragging
